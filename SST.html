Why people travelled to India from various parts of the world in ancient times?

History

Five motivations behind travelling to India in ancient times are:

1. Religious and spiritual exploration

India was the birth place of Hinduism and Buddhism and had many sacred sites like Bodh Gaya,
Varanasi and Sarnath.

2 .Trade and commerce

India was well known for its wealth and resources, including textiles, spices and goods.

3. Cultural and exploration

India's cultural diversity, with its languages, customs and traditions, explores.

4. Other reasons people travelled to India are:

To attend games and festivals.

For military purposes.

To study Indian cultural, society and literature.

The key reasons for travel in history are:

1. Trade

2. Religion

3. Exploration

4. Adventure

5. Learning

6. Visiting friends or relatives

Geography

Geographical features of India

1. Himalayan mountains:

The northernmost part of India, the Himalayas stretch from Kashmir to Arunachal Pradesh and are divided into three parts:
the Greater Himalayas, the Lesser Himalayas and the Outer Himalayas.

2. Peninsular Plateau:

the oldest physical division of India, the Peninsular Plateau is made up of the Deccan Plateau and Central Highlands.
The Western Ghats are located on the western edge of the plateau.

3. The Great Thar Desert;

One of the worlds largest Deserts, the Great Thar Desert is located in the northwest of India

4. Coastal Plains:

The east coastal plains run from West Bengal to Tamil Nadu, and the western coastal plains
run from Gujarat to Karnataka and Kerala.
