Math Project Topic - Indian Mathematician Aryabhatta

Introduction to Aryabhatta

Aryabhatta also know as the father of Indian mathematics. Aryabhatta was also an Indian astronomer.
He contributed significant work in science and mathematics and concluded a lot of theories at earth
rotation on its axis. One of his famous work in Aryabhatiya, this is written in Sanskrit language.
Aryabhatta was born in patliputra, which is now present Patna, Bihar state in the year 476. He completed
his studies Kusumpura institution ( Kulpara) He was also the head of the Nalanda University, Bihar.

Contribution of Aryabhatta to Mathematics

Aryabhatta made several contributions to mathematics inventions and theories. He is also called the king
of Indian mathematics. the important discoveries and theories made by Aryabhatta are the place value system
and 0, Trigonometry, Algebra, Approximation of pi and Indeterminate equations. Aryabhatta died in the
year 550 BCE in Patilputra only. The contributions made by Aryabhatta died in the year 550 BCE in Patilputra
are still used. Aryabhatta's contributions to mathematics were very helpful to the people. Aryabhatta's
formulas and theories lead the people more helpful to learn more concepts of mathematics.
